Pork and Chicken Adobo

Looking for the ultimate Filipino comfort food? Adobo may one of your choice, it is considered as the signature dish of the Philippines.

Chicken and Pork Adobo combines two classics into one delectable dish. Braised low and slow in vinegar, soy sauce, and spices, it is bursting with savory, garlicky flavors that are sure to hit the spot!

This dish is like making both Chicken Adobo and Pork Adobo in one pot. This dish is best prepared for either lunch or dinner.


Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 lbs pork belly, chopped
  • 1 1/2 lbs chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 4 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 1 head garlic, slightly crushed
  • 6 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 2 teaspoons brown sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil

Instructions:

  1. In a wide pan over medium heat, heat oil. Add onions and garlic and cook until softened.
  2. Add pork and cook, turning as needed, until lightly browned. Cook, stirring occasionally, for about 7 to 10 minutes.
  3. Add chicken and cook, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned and juices run clear.
  4. Add vinegar and bring to a boil, uncovered and without stirring, for about 2 to 3 minutes.
  5. Add soy sauce, water, and bay leaves. Continue to boil for about 2 to 3 minutes.
  6. Lower heat, cover, and cook for about 30 to 40 minutes or until meat is tender and sauce is reduced.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot with warm white rice.
